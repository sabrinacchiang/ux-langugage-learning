# Project Overview

## Project Title

Your Story: An AI-Powered Language Learning Companion

## Timeline

6-Week AI-Powered UX Design Capstone

## Problem

Adult language learners repeatedly return to language learning but rarely reach fluency.

Existing language learning tools are designed around habit formation, streaks, lesson completion, and gamified engagement. While these approaches can increase short-term activity, they often fail to support the psychological conditions required for long-term language acquisition and sustained motivation.

Research identified two critical conditions that are often missing:

### Emotional Safety

Learners need to feel safe attempting the language without fear of judgment, failure, punishment, or embarrassment.

When emotional safety is absent, learners disengage during practice.

### Identity Proximity

Learners need to maintain a vivid sense of who they are becoming as a future speaker of the language.

When that future identity becomes blurry or feels unattainable, motivation collapses and learners stop returning.

The result is a predictable cycle of starting, stalling, and restarting.

## Design Challenge

How might we bring the two conditions episodic adult learners are most missing — emotional safety to attempt the language without fear, and a sustained sense of becoming a speaker — into the fragmented, returnable moments of everyday life?

## Target Users

### Primary Persona: Maya (The Restarter)

Maya has attempted to learn Spanish multiple times through apps, classes, and tutors. She does not struggle with motivation. She struggles with maintaining momentum when progress feels invisible and disconnected from meaningful outcomes.

### Secondary Persona: Daniel (The Avoider)

Daniel wants to learn Portuguese to connect more deeply with his family, but anxiety emerges before meaningful progress can occur. Speaking feels evaluative, which causes him to withdraw before confidence can develop.

## Core Insight

The problem is not that adult learners lack motivation.

The problem is that existing tools fail to create the conditions that allow language learning to feel safe, meaningful, and connected to a learner's identity.

## Proposed Solution

Rather than teaching a fixed curriculum, the product helps learners prepare for meaningful moments in their lives through language.

The experience begins with an AI conversation that uncovers:

- Who the learner wants to become
- Why the language matters to them
- The people they want to connect with
- The situations they want to navigate

The system then generates personalized, moment-driven practice based on those real-life contexts.

Instead of asking:

"What lesson should I do next?"

The product asks:

"What real-life moment am I preparing for?"

## North Star

The goal of this product is not to help users complete lessons.

The goal is to help users prepare for meaningful moments in their lives through language.

Every design decision is evaluated against a single question:

> Does this help users feel prepared for a real moment that matters to them?

If not, it is deprioritized.

## Guiding Principle

The product is not trying to become a better curriculum.

It is trying to become a better companion.

A curriculum teaches language.

A companion helps someone prepare for their life.
